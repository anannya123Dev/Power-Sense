How to Use
Five simple steps to get the most out of PowerSense.
1

Open the Dashboard

The Dashboard is your live view. Numbers update every 5 seconds automatically. Just open it and watch — no clicking needed.

💡 Keep this open on a spare screen for a permanent live power display of your home.
2

Read the Status Banner

The coloured bar at the top of the dashboard tells you instantly whether usage is Low, Medium, or High. Green is good. Red means act now.

💡 If it turns red, switch off the AC — it is usually the biggest power consumer.
3

Toggle Devices On or Off

Each device in the table has a toggle switch. Flip a device off to instantly see how much power and money you save without it running.

💡 Toggle off the AC and watch the bill estimate drop in real time.
4

Browse History

Click History in the top menu to see all past readings. Use the date pickers to filter a specific day, week, or month.

💡 Check at month-end to compare which days had the highest usage.
5

Export Your Data

Click Export in the menu to download all readings as a CSV spreadsheet. Open in Excel or Google Sheets for your own analysis.

💡 Useful to share with your electrician if you suspect a faulty appliance.
The Numbers
What each value on the dashboard actually means — in plain English.
⚡

Power — Watts (W)

How much electricity a device is using right now. Think of it like water flow — higher watts means more electricity. A bulb uses ~10 W, an AC uses ~1500 W.

P = V × I
🔋

Units — kWh

What your electricity bill is based on. 1 unit = 1000 watts running for 1 hour. Running a 1000 W AC for 1 hour = 1 unit on your meter.

kWh = W × h ÷ 1000
🔌

Voltage (V)

Electrical pressure in your supply. In India it should always be 220–230 V. Drops or spikes can damage appliances.

India standard: 230 V
〰️

Current — Amperes (A)

The flow of electricity through a device. A fan uses ~0.5 A, an AC uses ~5 A. Higher current means more power consumed.

I = P ÷ V
🧮

Hybrid Power

Average of two readings — one calculated by the system (V × I) and one directly from the sensor. Using both gives a more accurate and stable result.

Hybrid = (calc + api) ÷ 2
🤖

ML Predicted Power

Our Random Forest model analyses voltage, current, and hybrid readings to predict total power consumption. It gets smarter as more data is collected.

FAQ
Common questions answered simply.
Why does the power number keep changing? +
The system reads sensor data every 5 seconds and simulates real-world fluctuations. In actual homes, power changes constantly as devices cycle on and off throughout the day.
What is Hybrid Power and why do we use it? +
Hybrid Power is the average of the sensor-reported value and the system-calculated value (V × I). Sensors can drift and raw formulas can have noise. Averaging both gives a more stable, accurate reading — a technique used in real energy meters.
How is my electricity bill calculated? +
Bill = (Total Watts ÷ 1000) × Hours × Tariff rate (₹ per unit). The app uses ₹6.5 per unit by default. Use the Bill Calculator tab to enter your actual tariff from your electricity bill.
What does the machine learning model do? +
A Random Forest model is trained on historical voltage, current, and hybrid readings. It learns usage patterns and predicts total power consumption more accurately than a single formula, and classifies it as Low, Medium, or High.
The date filter in History is not showing results — why? +
Make sure the From and To dates you enter actually contain data. If you just started the app today, all readings are from today only. Let it run for a few days and then the date filter becomes more useful.
Can I connect this to real smart plugs? +
Yes! Replace the simulated data in data_collector.py with real API calls from Shelly, Sonoff, or Tuya smart plugs. These provide voltage, current, and power over a local HTTP API. The rest of the system works exactly the same.
